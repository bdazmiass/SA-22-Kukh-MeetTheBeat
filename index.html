STRAY KIDS

Stray Kids (스트레이 키즈 Seuteurei Kijeu), shortened as SKZ (스키즈), is a South Korean boy group under JYP Entertainment, formed through the 2017 reality show of the same name. The group officially debuted on March 25, 2018 with their debut showcase, followed by the release of their first mini album I am NOT on March 26.

Short Information

The leader, Bang Chan, personally selected each member to be a part of the band before filming the eponymous 2017 reality television show, which is unusual in K-pop where that authority is usually held by the agency's executives and creative directors. The band released their unofficial debut extended play (EP) Mixtape in January 2018 and officially debuted on March 25 with the EP I Am Not, which was followed by the EPs I Am Who and I Am You, completing the I Am EP series. The Clé trilogy, consisting of Clé 1: Miroh, Clé 2: Yellow Wood, and Clé: Levanter, was released in 2019.

The band's first studio album, Go Live (2020), became its first platinum-certified album by the Korea Music Content Association (KMCA). That year, Stray Kids made their Japanese debut with the compilation album SKZ2020, which was released through Epic Records Japan. Their debut Japanese single "Top" debuted atop the Oricon Singles Chart, the fourth foreign male artist to do so with their first single.

In 2021, Stray Kids' second studio album Noeasy became its first million-selling album. After signing with Republic Records for promotions in the United States in 2022, the band released their EPs Oddinary, Maxident (both 2022), Rock-Star (2023), and Ate (2024); their studio albums 5-Star (2023) & Karma (2025); and their first mixtape Hop (2024). These seven releases peaked at number one on the US Billboard 200, making them the first act to debut at the top of the Billboard 200 with their first-seven-charted albums. The KMCA certified 5-Star five million in album sales, making Stray Kids the third group to achieve this in Korea. In 2023, the band appeared on Time's list of Next Generation Leaders. As of December 2024, Stray Kids has sold over 31 million albums, both Korean and Japanese releases.

Latest Releases

KARMA

Karma is the fourth Korean-language studio album. It was released on August 21, 2025, through JYP Entertainment and Republic Records. The album is the follow-up to the group's previous Korean extended play Ate (2024), and serves as their first full-length Korean studio album since 5-Star (2023).

The group's in-house production 3Racha participated in writing all tracks of Karma, alongside collaborators DallasK, Jun2, Millionboy, and Versachoi. The lead single "Ceremony" supported the album.

Commercially, Karma topped the national album charts in South Korea, Austria, Belgium, France, Germany, etc.

Hollow

Hollow is the fourth Japanese-language extended play[A] (fifteenth overall). It was released by Epic Records Japan on June 18, 2025, seven months after its predecessor Giant (2024). The EP was supported by the lead single of the same name.

HOP

Hop (Korean: 合; RR: Hap; lit. 'unity') is the first mixtape released on December 13, 2024. Marketed as the group's "special album" and first "SKZhop Hiptape" record, the mixtape features songs of "Stray Kids' only new genre", and a guest appearance from Canadian rapper Tablo.
